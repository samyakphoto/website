About Snow Leopard Expedition 2026

High Altitude Himalayan Wildlife in Winter


In the High Mountains of Himalaya, only the toughest and hardest animals survive. The Snow Leopard is the most beautiful Big Cat that rules here!

Snow Leopard (panthera uncia) is an Apex Predator of the Himalayan Mountains.
During winter, The Big Cat that inhabits the high altitude alpine slopes and deep valleys, come down due to excessive snow at the top.
The place offers a great insight into the cold, himalayan wildlife at its peak winter. Apart from the Snow Leopard, we would also have a good chance to dvelve into the life of Red Fox, Ibex, Blue Sheeps and birds like Himalayan Griffon, Lammergier, Golden Eagle that call this cold desert its home.
The place also offers breathtaking landscapes, an adventurous road travel and memories that would last us a lifetime!

Please note as part of responsible and ethical photography, we do not encourage using baits/lure/bird calls to approach wildlife for our photography.





Day 1 : Chandigarh - Narkanda. Overnight stay.
Day 2 : Narkanda - Kalpa. Continue our onwards journey towards Kalpa for overnight stay. In the Evening, presentation and discussions on basics of Himalayan Wildlife Photography followed by QnA.
Day 3 : Kalpa - Kaza/Kibber. Rest and acclimatization. This is our home for next few days.
Day 4-8 : Kibber - Snow Leopard tracking around the valleys and nearby mountains. Daily evening review session of photographs taken through the day.
Day 9 : Kibber - Kalpa. Return to our base in Kalpa after happy memories from Spiti Valley.
Day 10 : Kalpa - Chandigarh for departure.

Expedition Mentor

Samyak Kaninde

Samyak has been visiting Spiti Valley since 2014 in search of Snow Leopards. He is most experinced in tracking and photographing Himalayan wildlife in the best way possible.
With keen sense of light and animal behaviour, his images stand out for their creative compositions, drama and showing a world of animals in its truest sense.

Through his limited photography tours, he want to introduce the nature lovers to the most responsible and ethical ways to observe and photograph wildlife.

His photo stories and nature writings are published in various magazines, newspapers, coffee table books & nature education materials. He has an experience of more than a decade in nature and wildlife photography.
